About the Programme

The Hydro Sprout Initiative (HSI)

The Hydro Sprout Initiative (HSI) is a groundbreaking hydroponic community development program aimed at fostering sustainable agriculture, improving food security, and empowering local communities through modern and sustainable farming practices.

This innovative project is driven by a multi-stakeholder partnership, with PTTEP as the main sponsor, the Bintulu Development Authority (BDA) as the land contributor and collaborator, University Putra Malaysia providing technical expertise and as the project coordinator, and the Lions Club of Bintulu Kidurong acting as the financial steward and implementer.

By leveraging hydroponic technology, the program aims to address local food supply challenges, create entrepreneurship opportunities, and uplift low-income communities in Bintulu — ultimately contributing to the socio-economic well-being of the community.

The Technology

What is Hydroponics?

Hydroponic farming is an innovative method of growing plants without soil, using nutrient-rich water solutions to deliver essential nutrients directly to plant roots. By providing a controlled growing environment, hydroponic systems enable faster plant growth, higher yields, and year-round cultivation regardless of weather. Compared to conventional agriculture, hydroponics can use up to 90% less water, requires less land, and minimises the need for pesticides — making it a sustainable, resource-efficient approach to food production, especially well suited to urban areas where land is limited and food security matters.

Benefits of Hydroponic Farming

A smarter, cleaner way to grow food — better for you, for the farmer, and for the planet.

Saves Water

Uses up to 90% less water than soil farming through closed-loop recirculation.

No Pesticides

Grown in clean, controlled conditions, so produce is naturally pesticide-free.

Year-Round Harvest

Climate-controlled growing means fresh produce in every season, rain or shine.

Faster Growth

Nutrients delivered straight to the roots let plants grow up to twice as fast.

Space-Efficient

Vertical, soil-free systems grow more food in less space, even in urban areas.

Nutrient-Rich & Fresh

Optimised nutrition and same-day freshness mean healthier, better-tasting produce.

Looking Ahead

Long-Term Impact

Entrepreneurial Ecosystem

Successful hydroponic businesses can expand, diversify crops, and become suppliers, consultants or trainers — growing a localised economy.

Food Security

A network of local farms shortens supply chains, builds resilience, and shields the community from global food price volatility.

Job Creation

New ventures create jobs in production, packaging, sales, marketing, logistics and retail — rippling out to other local industries.

Knowledge Transfer

Graduates mentoring new participants creates a continuous cycle of learning, leadership and stronger social bonds.

Urban Greening

Rooftops, vacant lots and indoor spaces can be transformed into productive farms, making cities greener and land use more efficient.

Lower Carbon Footprint

Up to 90% less water and locally grown food reduce resource use and cut transport emissions — supporting global environmental goals.

Once established, HSI can serve as a scalable model for other communities locally and globally — adapting hydroponic entrepreneurship to address food security, economic empowerment and sustainability everywhere.
