About the Program

The Hydro Sprout Initiative

The Hydro Sprout Initiative (HSI) is a groundbreaking hydroponic community development program aimed at fostering sustainable agriculture, improving food security, and empowering local communities in Bintulu through modern farming practices.

It is driven by a multi-stakeholder partnership — with PTTEP as the main sponsor, the Bintulu Development Authority (BDA) as land contributor and collaborator, Universiti Putra Malaysia providing technical expertise and project coordination, and the Lions Club of Bintulu Kidurong acting as financial steward and implementer.

By leveraging hydroponic technology, the program addresses local food-supply challenges, creates entrepreneurship opportunities, and uplifts low-income communities — contributing to the socio-economic well-being of Bintulu.

Three goals at the heart of the Hydro Sprout Initiative.

Empower the Community

What is Hydroponic Farming?

Hydroponics grows plants without soil, delivering nutrient-rich water directly to the roots in a controlled environment. It enables faster growth, higher yields, and year-round cultivation — using up to 90% less water, less land, and minimal pesticides. A smarter, cleaner way to grow food — better for you, the farmer, and the planet.

💧

Saves Water

Uses up to 90% less water than soil farming through closed-loop recirculation.

🚫

No Pesticides

Grown in clean, controlled conditions, so produce is naturally pesticide-free.

📅

Year-Round Harvest

Climate-controlled growing means fresh produce in every season, rain or shine.

⚡

Faster Growth

Nutrients delivered straight to the roots let plants grow up to twice as fast.

🏙️

Space-Efficient

Vertical, soil-free systems grow more food in less space, even in urban areas.

🥗

Nutrient-Rich & Fresh

Optimised nutrition and same-day freshness mean healthier, better-tasting produce.

Long-Term Impact

As the program matures, HSI aims to build a lasting, self-sustaining local economy.

🌐 Entrepreneurial Ecosystem

Successful hydroponic businesses expand, diversify, and become suppliers, consultants and trainers within the community.

🥬 Local Supply & Food Security

Shorter supply chains make food systems more resilient and less dependent on external markets.

💼 Job Creation

New ventures create jobs in production, packaging, sales, logistics, and retail — a ripple effect of economic growth.

🤝 Knowledge Transfer

Graduates mentor new participants, creating a continuous cycle of learning, leadership and stronger social bonds.

🏙️ Urban Greening

Unused rooftops, lots and indoor spaces transform into productive farming zones — greener, more efficient land use.

🌍 Lower Carbon Footprint

Efficient water use and locally-grown food reduce long-distance transport and conserve natural resources.

Once established, HSI can serve as a scalable model for other communities — locally and globally — adapting hydroponic entrepreneurship to address their own challenges.

A 3-Year Journey

HSI Activities

From classroom to market — empowering B40 and low-income households through hydroponic farming and entrepreneurship.

1

Technical Training

Classroom and practical training from hydroponic experts — system setup, nutrient management, environmental control, and farm maintenance.

2

Hands-On Farming

Participants manage hydroponic plots — planting, monitoring growth, maintaining systems, and solving real-world challenges under expert guidance.

3

Business & Product Development

Training in business planning, cost management, branding, packaging, and sales — supported by this online marketplace platform.

4

Planting, Harvesting & Sales

Participants cultivate, harvest, market and sell their produce — gaining experience across the complete farm-to-market value chain.

5

Mentorship & Graduation

Outstanding graduates become mentors for new participants, with starter kits and support to establish their own commercial hydroponic farms.

The Outcome

Improved livelihoods, stronger food security, new jobs, and a new generation of sustainable agropreneurs in Bintulu.
